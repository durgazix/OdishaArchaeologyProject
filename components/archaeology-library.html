LIBRARY

Odisha State Archaeology Department has one of the finest archaeological libraries in the country, housing over 13,300 books on Archaeology, Anthropology, Art, History, Epigraphy and Palaeography authored by reputed foreign and Indian scholars and experts. The collection includes rare volumes, research publications, excavation reports, and comprehensive documentation of Odisha's rich cultural heritage.

The library serves as a vital resource center for researchers, scholars, students, and heritage enthusiasts who wish to explore the archaeological wealth of Odisha. It contains extensive materials on temple architecture, ancient inscriptions, Buddhist monuments, tribal culture, and the maritime history of ancient Kalinga. Special collections feature monographs on Odishan art forms, medieval manuscripts, and archaeological survey reports dating back several decades.

Our library is regularly updated with new acquisitions, journals, and digital resources to support ongoing research in archaeology and heritage conservation. Visitors can access reference materials, periodicals, and photographic archives that document significant archaeological discoveries across Odisha. The reading room provides a conducive environment for serious academic study and research work.
